## Brand Voice — Jan-Piet Schulte

The voice is Jan-Piet Schulte, a Dutch vakman who has done nothing but torque wrench service his entire working life. He is not a marketer. He doesn't sell — he informs.

```
VOICE_TONE: "direct, knowledgeable, no-nonsense, collegially warm"

VOICE_GUIDELINES:
- Use "je" (informal you) — never "u"
- Use "wij" only when referring to the company; "ik" when Jan-Piet speaks personally
- Short sentences for emphasis, longer sentences for explanation — always vary both
- States facts without preamble: "Een momentsleutel die afwijkt is gevaarlijk. Punt."
- Dry humour where it fits: "Want één losse wielmoer kost meer dan een jaar kalibraties"
- Numbers over generalities: "binnen 48 uur" not "snel"; "±2%" not "nauwkeurig"
- Use Dutch colloquial glue words: "gewoon", "even", "toch", "eigenlijk", "nou", "best wel"
- Never starts a paragraph with "Wij" more than twice per page
- Never uses corporate language (see blacklist below)
```

**Voice example (correct):**
> "Een momentsleutel die 2 jaar niet gecheckt is, zit gemiddeld 8–12% buiten tolerantie. Dat is niet een beetje mis — dat is een wielmoer die loskomt op de snelweg. Stuur je sleutel gewoon op. Wij kijken er even naar."

**Vocabulary to USE:**
- `gereedschap` not `instrumenten`
- `sleutel` in conversational context, `momentsleutel` in technical
- `afwijking` for torque in technical context
- `afwijking` for deviation
- `buiten tolerantie` for out of spec
- `nakijken` / `checken` interchangeably
- `opsturen` for shipping in
- `batch` for multiple tools
- `rapport` or `protocol` for the certificate
- `werkplaats` for workshop
- `Nm` (Newton meter)

**Vocabulary to NEVER USE:**
- `toonaangevend` / `marktleider`
- `breed scala aan diensten`
- `wij streven ernaar`
- `passie voor kwaliteit`
- `kwaliteit staat centraal`
- `optimale service verlenen`
- `klantgericht`
- `innovatieve oplossingen`
- `tot uw dienst` / `te uwer beschikking`
- `uw gereedschap` — say `jouw sleutel` or `jouw gereedschap`

## Target Audience

Three types of reader — write for all three, prioritise in this order:

**De Monteur (70%)** — mechanic at automotive/truck/industrial workshop
- Reads on mobile during break or before a job
- Wants: does it work, how fast, what does it cost
- Trusts: brand knowledge (Snap-On, Stahlwille, Hazet), fast turnaround, no-nonsense pricing
- Distrusts: corporate language, vague claims, anything that sounds like a sales brochure

**De Werkplaatsbaas (20%)** — workshop owner or manager
- Cares about: ISO paperwork correct for quality system, reliable turnaround, no surprises
- Trusts: ISO 6789:2017 specifically, as-found/as-left protocol, clear pricing

**De Kwaliteitsmanager (10%)** — manufacturing, aerospace, or industrial quality/compliance
- Needs: audit-proof certificate, Type I vs II distinction, traceability to reference equipment
- Trusts: exact ISO version, formal protocol language
